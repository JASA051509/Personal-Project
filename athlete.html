Serbia
Tennis
Novak Djokovic
Novak Djokovic won his much-longed-for Olympic gold medal. In the men's singles tennis final on Sunday at Paris 2024 Olympics, he defeated Carlos Alcaraz. Djokovic took a nail-biting tiebreak 7-6, 7-6 with two intense sets to complete historic 'Golden Slam', that is, winning all four major titles plus Olympic gold. The record 24-time Grand Slam champion Serbian also added this gold to the bronze he won in Beijing 2008. Victory for the fierce and nearly three-hour battle marked a cross-generational clash between Djokovic and the 21-year-old Alcaraz. Of that victory, Djokovic was very emotional, overwhelmed by finally securing this title.
Brazil
Skateboarding
Rayssa Leal
From Brazil, Rayssa Leal of the country thrilled fans in the Women's Skateboarding Street event by providing an exciting end to the competition in at the Olympics in Paris 2024. When 16-year-old Leal tore her way to a bronze medal finish, moving up from 5th place for her second Olympic podium in a row, the final trick proved to be the difference for her going into third place.
India
Javeiln Throw
Neeraj Chopra
Neeraj Chopra won silver in the men's javelin throw on Friday at the 2024 Paris Olympics with his second-best career throw of 89.45m, failing to retain Tokyo 2020 gold as Pakistan's Arshad Nadeem soared to a new Olympic record of 92.97m. Thus, Chopra is a two-time Olympic medalist and adds silver to his gold in Tokyo 2020. He adds himself to the list of Indian athletes that have won medals in more than one Summer Games. In fact, he becomes the third athlete to win for India.
USA
Basketball
LeBron James
LeBron James is one of the favorites to bring home a third Olympic gold with Team USA as they count down to his 40th birthday at Paris 2024. He has become one of the driving forces of the team, making do much more than just scoring. In fact, for 12 years, the Olympics had been a dark blot in his five-time NBA champion calendar. LeBron's return has added a new level of vigor to the roster that has superstars like Kevin Durant and Stephen Curry. He has thrilled his teammates and his coach Steve Kerr by asserting that the want to lead and inspire is what struck him about LeBron. A winner, James is determined to win gold and points out that this win is much more a product of teamwork than personal triumph.
Algeria
Boxing
Imane Khelif
Imane Khelif of Algeria went home with a gold medal, taking home the women's 66kg boxing during Paris 2024 defeating Yang Liu of China, 5-0. Khelif, who made history by becoming Algeria's first female Olympic boxer at Tokyo 2020, had earlier won gold in the 2022 Mediterranean Games and the 2023 Arab Games. They were overwhelmed by their victory, saying that is a result of eight years of hard work and sacrifice. Yang who won gold at the 2023 World Championships said, "She is too strong. I was outplayed."
